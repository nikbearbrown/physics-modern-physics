## Figure-by-Figure Validation

### Fig 4.1 — Classical vs. Quantum Blackbody Curves
- **Type:** Statistical / quantitative. **Verdict:** Keep — central to chapter.
- **SCOPE:** Two curves on the same axes. X: wavelength (nm, ~100 to 3000). Y: spectral radiance (W·m⁻³·sr⁻¹·nm⁻¹, starts at zero). Classical Rayleigh-Jeans curve rising toward infinity at short λ. Planck curve peaking and rolling off. One temperature, ~5800 K (solar-relevant). Mark the divergence visually.
- **P:** Okabe-Ito vermillion (classical, "wrong") + sky-blue (Planck, "right"). Y-axis starts at zero. Note the visible spectrum range with light gray vertical band.
- **Caption hook:** "The classical curve goes to infinity. The data does not. The disagreement at short wavelengths is the 'ultraviolet catastrophe' — and the first crack in classical physics."

### Fig 4.2 — Why High-Frequency Modes Go Dark (Minimum-Wage Analogy)
- **Type:** Conceptual map / mechanism diagram. **Verdict:** Keep — useful intuition pump.
- **SCOPE:** Bar chart visualization. X: frequency (low to high). Y: energy per quantum (hf, linearly increasing). Horizontal line at thermal energy k_BT. Modes below the line "active" (filled bars); modes above the line "dark" (empty/grayed bars).
- **P:** 8-10 frequency bins. Filled bars Okabe-Ito sky-blue; dark bars light gray with cross-hatch. Threshold line vermillion.
- **Caption:** Reinforces author's wage-budget metaphor without belaboring it.

### Fig 4.3 — Photon Energy vs. Wavelength (Photoelectric Threshold)
- **Type:** Statistical / quantitative. **Verdict:** Keep — but **fix caption truncation** ("Photon energy (eV) vs").
- **SCOPE:** X: wavelength (nm, 200–700, spanning UV to red). Y: photon energy (eV, ~1.7–6.2). One curve E = hc/λ (hyperbolic). Horizontal lines at work functions of common metals (calcium 2.71 eV, sodium 2.28 eV, cesium 2.1 eV, gold 5.1 eV). Visible spectrum colored strip along x-axis.
- **P:** Single curve. 4 metal threshold lines (Okabe-Ito sky-blue, orange, yellow, gray — avoid red/green pair). Where each metal threshold intersects the photon-energy curve, drop a vertical line marking the corresponding cutoff wavelength.
- **Caption:** "Each metal has a minimum photon energy below which no electrons emerge — and that minimum is per photon, not per beam. Intensity is irrelevant; frequency is everything. Calcium needs UV. Cesium responds to green."

### Fig 4.4 — Comet Tail Geometry (Radiation Pressure)
- **Type:** Annotated example (photograph or diagram). **Verdict:** Keep.
- **SCOPE:** Comet orbit ellipse around Sun. Two positions: (a) approaching Sun and (b) receding. In both, tail points away from Sun (not opposite to motion). Sun at center with radial arrows showing photon momentum flux.
- **P:** 6 components: Sun, comet at position A, comet at position B, motion vectors, tail vectors. Okabe-Ito yellow for Sun, sky-blue for comet, vermillion for tail. Caption fixes the truncation.

### Fig 4.5 — Compton Scattering Geometry
- **Type:** Mechanism cross-section. **Verdict:** Keep.
- **SCOPE:** Incoming photon (short wavelength) strikes electron at rest. Scattered photon at angle θ (longer wavelength). Electron recoils at angle φ. Wavelength shift Δλ = (h/mc)(1 − cos θ) shown in callout.
- **P:** 6 components: incoming photon (wavy arrow, short period), electron (filled dot), scattered photon (wavy arrow, longer period), recoil electron, angles labeled. Sky-blue for incoming, orange for scattered, black for electron. The wavelength change must be visually obvious in the wavy-arrow period.

### Fig 4.6 — Single-Photon Double-Slit Buildup
- **Type:** Sequential / time-series panels. **Verdict:** Keep — pedagogical centerpiece.
- **SCOPE:** Four panels showing detector accumulation: (a) 10 photons (random scatter), (b) 100 photons (faint fringe hint), (c) 1,000 photons (clear fringes emerging), (d) 100,000 photons (full interference pattern). Each photon is a single dot.
- **P:** 4 horizontal panels. White background, Okabe-Ito sky-blue dots. Identical scale and slit positions in each panel. No red/green.
- **Caption:** "Each photon lands at one point — particle. The pattern is interference — wave. The same photons do both. No classical mental picture accommodates this; the formalism does."

### Fig 4.7 — Hydrogen Emission Spectrum
- **Type:** Annotated spectrum strip. **Verdict:** Keep.
- **SCOPE:** Horizontal strip of the visible spectrum (continuous gradient as background). Four bright vertical emission lines at 410 nm (violet, faint), 434 nm (violet), 486 nm (cyan), 656 nm (red). Each line labeled with wavelength and Balmer-series notation (Hα, Hβ, Hγ, Hδ).
- **P:** 6 components: 4 emission lines + scale axis + series labels. Bright lines on the rainbow background. No additional Okabe-Ito needed (lines are their natural colors). Caption notes: nothing between, and that's the discovery.
