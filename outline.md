## Introduction

The 20th century broke classical mechanics in two places: relativity (large velocities, large masses) and quantum (small scales). This volume traces both, plus their consequences from stars to particle physics to cosmology.

## Chapters

1. **Special Relativity** — D3 Lorentz transformation animator; time dilation, length contraction, twin paradox.
2. **The Quantum Revolution — Light as Particles** — D3 photoelectric effect + UV catastrophe; blackbody radiation, Planck's hypothesis.
3. **Atomic Physics — The Bohr Model and Beyond** — D3 spectral lines animator; energy levels, emission/absorption, de Broglie.
4. **The Sun as a Nuclear Powerhouse** — D3 solar interior + proton-proton chain; stellar structure, neutrinos, helioseismology.
5. **Radioactivity and Nuclear Physics** — D3 decay chain animator; half-life, binding energy curve, iron peak.
6. **Medical Applications of Nuclear Physics** — D3 dose/decay visualizer; PET, MRI, radiation therapy, dose controversies.
7. **The Death of Stars** — D3 stellar remnant router; Chandrasekhar limit, neutron stars, supernovae, pulsars.
8. **Black Holes and Curved Spacetime** — D3 geodesic visualizer; event horizon, Schwarzschild radius, gravitational waves, Hawking radiation.
9. **Particle Physics and the Standard Model** — D3 particle explorer; quarks, leptons, bosons, what the model still doesn't explain.
10. **The Big Bang and Cosmology** — D3 Hubble diagram + CMB spectrum; expanding universe, dark matter, dark energy.
11. **Life in the Universe and the Frontiers of Physics** — D3 Drake equation interactive; open problems, bridge to QM+1.
